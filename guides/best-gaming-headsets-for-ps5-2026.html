PS5 Headset FAQ

Does any headset work on PS5, or do I need a special one?

Any USB or 2.4GHz wireless headset works on PS5. Bluetooth is blocked during gameplay on PS5 (Sony's design choice for latency reasons). For the best Tempest 3D AudioTech experience, use a USB-connected headset — the Pulse 3D and Pulse Elite get the deepest integration, but any USB 2.4GHz headset like the HS80 or Cloud Alpha Wireless works well.

Is the Pulse 3D worth it vs saving money on a wired headset?

Yes, for most PS5 players. The native wireless integration and Tempest 3D AudioTech support make the Pulse 3D the natural companion for PS5. A wired headset connected via the 3.5mm controller jack still gets Tempest 3D Audio, but with stereo signal quality — not the custom-tuned drivers the Pulse 3D was built with. The $99 Pulse 3D is hard to beat for the PS5-specific experience.

What is Tempest 3D AudioTech and does it work with all headsets?

Tempest 3D AudioTech is Sony's 3D spatial audio engine built into the PS5 — it simulates sounds from above, below, and all around you. It works with any headset connected via USB, 2.4GHz dongle, or 3.5mm — stereo headsets get an HRTF-processed 3D effect. The effect is best on headphones tuned for it (Pulse 3D, Pulse Elite) but is genuinely impressive even on third-party headsets.

Can I use an Xbox headset on PS5?

Sometimes — headsets with a universal 3.5mm jack work on both via the controller. Headsets using a proprietary Xbox wireless dongle do not work on PS5. If you need a headset that works on both PS5 and Xbox, use a 2.4GHz USB dongle headset (like the HS80 or Cloud Alpha Wireless) which includes dongles for both platforms.

Does the PS5 support Bluetooth headsets?

The PS5 officially blocks Bluetooth audio during gameplay because Sony prioritizes zero-latency audio. In September 2023, Sony added Bluetooth audio support via the PlayStation Link USB adapter (sold separately, ~$30) — this gives wireless headsets not in the Pulse line the ability to connect via Bluetooth with low latency. Otherwise, USB dongle headsets are the go-to wireless option.
