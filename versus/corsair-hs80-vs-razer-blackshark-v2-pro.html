Corsair HS80 RGB Wireless vs Razer BlackShark V2 Pro: The Wireless Headset Face-Off

Two of the most recommended wireless gaming headsets in the $150–$200 range — and they're more different than their price tags suggest. The Corsair HS80 leans into immersive audio and mic clarity. The BlackShark V2 Pro leans into competitive soundstage and marathon battery life. Here's who should buy which.

Quick Verdict
BlackShark V2 Pro for competitive; HS80 for value + mic clarity
The BlackShark V2 Pro's TriForce Titanium drivers and 70-hour battery are exceptional, and the competitive soundstage is the best at this price point. But the Corsair HS80 undercuts it by $50, has a class-leading microphone, and works flawlessly with PS5. If you play competitively on PC, pay the extra $50 for the BlackShark. If you're on PS5 or mic quality is the priority, the HS80 wins.

Head-to-Head: Category by Category

Competitive Sound
BlackShark V2 Pro
The TriForce Titanium 50mm drivers in the BlackShark are tuned specifically for positional audio — the driver is split into three zones for bass, mid, and treble. In FPS games, footsteps, reloads, and directional cues are noticeably clearer. The HS80's Dolby Atmos implementation is good for immersive games but slightly less surgical for competitive play.
Microphone Quality
Corsair HS80
The HS80's omni-directional microphone is the best in class at this price — voice clarity is excellent and background noise handling is better than the BlackShark's HyperClear Supercardioid mic. Streamers and players who prioritize voice quality should note this. The BlackShark's mic is good, not great.
Battery Life
BlackShark V2 Pro — significantly
70 hours on the BlackShark vs 20 hours on the HS80. This is a massive gap — the BlackShark needs charging roughly 3× less frequently. For players who game heavily and don't want to think about battery, this alone is a compelling differentiator.
Platform Support
Corsair HS80
The HS80 officially supports PC and PS5 with full wireless. The BlackShark V2 Pro supports PC, PS5, Xbox, Switch, and mobile via dual wireless (2.4GHz + Bluetooth). Slight edge to BlackShark for broader platform coverage, but HS80 is still excellent for the most common use case (PC/PS5).
Value
Corsair HS80
At $50 less, the HS80 delivers 80% of the BlackShark's performance. For most gamers that ratio justifies choosing it. The BlackShark's extra $50 buys you 50 more hours of battery, a better competitive soundstage, and broader platform compatibility — real improvements, but incremental.
Build Quality
Tie
Both are built to last with quality plastic frames, plush earcups, and adjustable headbands that hold their position well. The BlackShark feels marginally more premium, but you'd need to hold both at the same time to notice. Neither will feel cheap coming out of the box.
