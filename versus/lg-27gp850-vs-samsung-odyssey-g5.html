LG 27GP850-B vs Samsung Odyssey G5 32": IPS Precision vs VA Immersion

Two of the most recommended 1440p gaming monitors — but they're fundamentally different products. The LG 27GP850-B is a 27" IPS at 165Hz designed for competitive gaming. The Samsung Odyssey G5 is a 32" curved VA at 165Hz designed for immersive play. The $100 price gap is almost secondary — the real question is which type of panel fits your use case.

Quick Verdict
LG for competitive FPS; Samsung for immersive single-player and RPG
These monitors target different players. The LG 27GP850's IPS panel gives better color accuracy, wider viewing angles, and faster pixel response — critical for competitive shooters. The Samsung Odyssey G5's VA panel delivers deeper blacks, higher contrast, and a 32" curved screen better suited for single-player games and productivity. Buy the LG if you primarily play FPS competitively. Buy the Samsung if you want immersive gaming across varied genres at a larger size.

Head-to-Head: Category by Category

Panel Type & Color
LG 27GP850-B (IPS)
IPS panels have wider viewing angles and more accurate color reproduction out of the box. The LG 27GP850's Nano IPS panel covers 98% DCI-P3 — exceptional color accuracy that makes games look vivid from any angle. The Samsung's VA panel has slightly narrower viewing angles and colors shift when viewed off-center. For content creation or playing at non-standard angles, IPS is meaningfully better.
Contrast & Black Levels
Samsung Odyssey G5 (VA)
VA panels produce meaningfully deeper blacks than IPS — typically 3,000:1 contrast vs 1,000:1 for IPS. In dark scenes (horror games, space games, night environments), the Samsung's blacks look genuinely dark while the LG's blacks look like dark grey. For atmospheric single-player games, this difference is visible and impactful. For bright competitive games, it matters less.
Motion Clarity & Response
LG 27GP850-B
IPS panels have faster pixel response times than VA, and the LG 27GP850 is particularly fast — 1ms GtG with its Overclock mode. VA panels historically suffer from "smearing" on dark-to-dark pixel transitions, making moving objects look blurry in dark scenes. Samsung has improved this in recent generations, but the LG still edges it for pure motion clarity. In competitive shooters, this matters.
Screen Size
Samsung Odyssey G5
32" vs 27" is a substantial difference at typical desk distances (60–80cm). The Samsung's 32" 1440p gives 92 PPI — still sharp, and the larger canvas is more immersive for single-player games. The LG's 27" 1440p gives 108 PPI — noticeably crisper. Closer sitting distances favor the 27"; further away favor the 32". Measure your setup before deciding.
Curvature
Samsung Odyssey G5 (1000R)
The Samsung has an aggressive 1000R curvature — very noticeable and genuinely immersive for RPG, adventure, and sim games. The LG is flat. Curvature preference is subjective: competitive players often prefer flat for consistent pixel geometry in shooters, while immersive gamers prefer curved for the wraparound feel. Neither is objectively better — this is the most personal choice in the comparison.
Value
LG 27GP850-B
At $249, the LG 27GP850-B delivers Nano IPS, 165Hz, and 1ms response at a price that was impossible for this panel quality two years ago. The Samsung at $349 is fair for a 32" 1440p curved VA, but you're paying for size and curvature — not superior panel technology. If the price gap is a factor, the LG delivers more performance per dollar.
