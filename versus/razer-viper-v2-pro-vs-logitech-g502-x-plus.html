Razer Viper V2 Pro vs Logitech G502 X Plus

The ultimate wireless mouse dilemma: 58g ultralight FPS specialist with optical switches, or 89g feature-loaded powerhouse with 13 buttons and 130-hour battery?

Quick Verdict

Razer Viper V2 Pro if you want the definitive ultralight wireless FPS mouse — 58g, optical switches, ambidextrous shape, and a sensor that matches anything on the market for $10 less. Logitech G502 X Plus if versatility and button count drive your purchase — 13 programmable buttons, 130-hour battery, magnetic charging, and LIGHTFORCE hybrid switches make it the best all-purpose wireless mouse for MMO, MOBA, and power users.

6-Category Breakdown

Sensor & Tracking

Both flawless — Focus Pro 30K has a higher ceiling, HERO 25K excels on efficiency

The Razer Focus Pro 30K and Logitech HERO 25K are two of the finest gaming sensors ever produced. Real-world performance is indistinguishable at any DPI setting a human can meaningfully use — both track with zero smoothing, acceleration, or jitter on cloth and hard pads. The Focus Pro 30K carries a higher 30,000 DPI ceiling and includes asymmetric cutoff adjustment for precise lift-off customization, giving it a marginal edge for players who dial in specific lift-off distances for their mousepad. The HERO 25K is renowned for its power efficiency, which contributes significantly to the G502 X Plus's exceptional 130-hour battery life. In practical use, neither sensor will be a performance bottleneck at any skill level — the 750 IPS tracking on the Viper V2 Pro and the equivalent tracking on the G502 X Plus both vastly exceed what any human wrist can generate. Sensor choice is not the decision point here.

Weight & Shape

31g gap is massive — Viper wins on weight, G502 X Plus wins on ergonomics

The 31g weight difference between these two mice is not a minor specification gap — it is one of the largest weight contrasts you will find between flagship wireless mice. At 58g, the Viper V2 Pro is one of the lightest wireless gaming mice available; at 89g the G502 X Plus sits firmly in the traditional "feature-rich" weight class. You will immediately feel this difference the moment you pick each mouse up. The Viper uses a flat, ambidextrous symmetrical shape with minimal contouring — it works for left or right hand, and suits claw, fingertip, and smaller-hand palm grippers. The G502 X Plus uses Logitech's refined ergonomic form: a pronounced right-handed hump, sculpted thumb rest, and multiple grip ridges designed specifically for right-handed palm grip players with medium to large hands. The shape ergonomics give the G502 X Plus a comfort edge for long sessions despite the higher weight; the Viper's shape is optimized for speed and versatility rather than sustained all-day comfort.

Wireless Performance

HyperSpeed vs LIGHTSPEED — functionally identical at 1ms; both 2.4GHz only

Razer HyperSpeed and Logitech LIGHTSPEED are both proprietary 2.4GHz wireless protocols rated at 1ms report rate. In competitive testing and professional esports use, neither protocol introduces perceptible latency compared to a wired connection. Both are reliable in environments with moderate wireless interference and maintain a stable connection within standard desktop distances. A notable feature limitation shared by both mice here: neither the Viper V2 Pro nor the G502 X Plus supports Bluetooth. Both require their respective USB nano receivers for operation. If multi-device Bluetooth connectivity is important to you, neither of these mice provides it — look instead at the Razer DeathAdder V3 Pro or similar dual-wireless options. For a single dedicated gaming PC, the lack of Bluetooth is not a practical limitation.

Battery Life

G502 X Plus dominates at 130hr vs 80hr — plus magnetic dock charging

The Logitech G502 X Plus's 130-hour battery life is exceptional — at 4 hours of gaming per day, that is roughly 32 days between charges. The Viper V2 Pro's 80-hour battery is still excellent and puts 20 days between charges at the same usage rate, but it is clearly outclassed. The more meaningful charging advantage for the G502 X Plus is its compatibility with Logitech's magnetic PowerPlay charging mat, which charges the mouse continuously while you use it — meaning you never have a dead battery in a G502 X Plus/PowerPlay setup. The Viper V2 Pro charges via USB-C only. Both mice can be used wired while charging with no degradation in performance. The G502 X Plus wins this category decisively both on raw battery capacity and the ecosystem charging advantage.

Buttons & Switches

G502 X Plus has 13 vs 5 — a completely different tool for genre-specific play

This is the category where these two mice diverge most dramatically. The Viper V2 Pro has 5 buttons: left click, right click, scroll wheel click, and two side buttons. Clean, minimal, zero excess. The G502 X Plus has 13 fully programmable buttons including a multi-function wheel with left/right tilt, a precision mode toggle, two side buttons, a DPI shift key, and additional function buttons near the thumb. For MMO and MOBA players who bind dozens of abilities to their mouse, the G502 X Plus is a fundamentally different workflow tool. For pure FPS play, 5 buttons is optimal — fewer opportunities for accidental inputs. Switch technology also differs: the Viper V2 Pro uses Razer's optical switches (pure light-beam actuation, instantaneous, light click) while the G502 X Plus uses LIGHTFORCE hybrid optical-mechanical switches (mechanical spring feel with optical trigger detection). Both are reliable and fast; optical clicks feel lighter while LIGHTFORCE feels more deliberate and tactile. Neither is objectively better for all players.

Value

Viper V2 Pro costs $10 less; G502 X Plus delivers more absolute features

At ~$120 the Viper V2 Pro is $10 less expensive than the G502 X Plus and delivers one of the best wireless ultralight FPS experiences available at any price. The G502 X Plus at ~$130 packs significantly more hardware: 13 buttons, 130-hour battery, LIGHTFORCE switches, magnetic charging compatibility, and a fully sculpted ergonomic form. On raw feature count, the G502 X Plus offers more per dollar. But value is use-case dependent: if you want the lightest competitive wireless mouse under $130, the Viper V2 Pro is exceptional value. If you want a do-everything wireless mouse with deep button customization and best-in-class battery life, the G502 X Plus earns its slight premium. Neither mouse represents poor value — they serve genuinely different needs at nearly the same price point.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Viper V2 Pro worth it over the G502 X Plus?

For competitive FPS players, yes — 58g, optical switches, and a clean 5-button layout make the Viper V2 Pro the sharper tool for speed-and-accuracy play, and it costs $10 less. For MMO, MOBA, or productivity users, no — the G502 X Plus's 13 buttons, 130-hour battery, LIGHTFORCE switches, and magnetic dock charging make it a far more capable all-purpose mouse. Know your use case and the answer is clear.

Which mouse is better for FPS?

The Razer Viper V2 Pro is better for FPS. Its 58g weight reduces wrist fatigue and inertia during aggressive flicks, the ambidextrous shape suits competitive hand positions, and the 5-button layout eliminates accidental side inputs during tense engagements. The Focus Pro 30K sensor is world-class. The G502 X Plus can be used for FPS — the HERO 25K is equally excellent — but the extra 31g and 8 additional buttons are weight and complexity you don't need for pure aim-based gameplay.

Can the G502 X Plus be used for competitive gaming despite its weight?

Yes — many players compete effectively on the G502 X Plus. The HERO 25K sensor and LIGHTSPEED wireless are both flawless for competitive play. The weight trade-off matters most for players used to sub-70g mice performing fast flicks at lower sensitivities. If you have trained on heavier mice or use higher sensitivity settings, the 89g will feel stable rather than sluggish. It is not the optimal competitive FPS tool, but it is capable in the right hands.

What are LIGHTFORCE switches?

LIGHTFORCE is Logitech's hybrid optical-mechanical switch technology. They combine a physical spring mechanism (for the familiar click feel) with an optical sensor for actuation detection — meaning the click registers via light beam rather than metal contact. This delivers the tactile satisfaction of mechanical clicks with the zero-debounce reliability and longevity of optical detection. LIGHTFORCE switches feel more deliberate and slightly heavier than Razer's pure optical switches, which some players prefer. They are rated for far longer lifespan than standard mechanical switches with no double-click failure mode.

How does the Viper V2 Pro compare to the Superlight 2?

The Viper V2 Pro and Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2 are the two main ultralight wireless flagships. The Viper V2 Pro is 2g lighter (58g vs 60g), costs roughly $40 less, and uses optical switches vs the Superlight 2's mechanical. The Superlight 2 has a subtly curved top that some prefer for palm grip. Sensor performance is effectively equal. Both are excellent — the choice comes down to shape preference, switch preference, and whether the Razer vs Logitech software ecosystem matters to you.
